# PhDPal-Daily-Science-Stories SOP

## 1. Positioning
Provide daily emotional support for researchers through one science-history slice story.
Target tone is light, human, and slightly gossipy.
All facts must be verifiable and traceable.

## 2. Themes
- Today birthday: focus on a person, their context, and one personality/habit/relationship slice.
- Today discovery: focus on an event, the process behind the result, and the context at the time.
- Fallback if no strong same-day event: use "this week in history" with a setback/recovery friction story.

## 3. Writing Standard
- Tell one slice or one process, not a full timeline biography or grand praise essay.
- Use colloquial and light language; mild roast is allowed.
- Do not use sensationalism, humiliation, or moral judgment.
- Contrast, dispute, or setbacks are a plus but do not force drama.
- Prefer reliability over theatrical conflict.

## 4. Required Elements (Flexible Structure)
1. Anchor:
   - Who is the person/event.
   - Why it is "today/this week" using date anchors like birthday, publication, lecture, patent, or date range.
2. Core facts:
   - State clearly what happened with checkable claims.
3. Concrete detail:
   - Include at least one non-encyclopedic but traceable detail showing real context or personality.
4. Emotional resonance:
   - Translate the history slice into common research emotion or structural friction.
5. Closing landing:
   - End with a takeaway emotion: understood, decompressed, or gently supported.
6. Sources:
   - Include 2-4 traceable sources.

## 5. Verification and Boundaries
- Use social media and aggregators only as leads.
- Verify key facts and details through authoritative channels.
- Anecdotes are allowed only when traceable.
- If uncertain, mark uncertainty explicitly or remove the claim.
- For disputes (priority, authorship, credit), present major versions with source support and avoid forced judgments.
- Keep facts and commentary separated.

## 7. Sources Citation Format
- Include 2-4 sources per story.
- Use normalized format:
  - Author, Title, Venue/Archive/Publisher, Year, Identifier (DOI/ISBN/archive id/institution page title).
- For anecdotes, append material type:
  - letters / diary / interview transcript / memoir / biography / archival collection.

## 8. Images
### 8.1 Image types
- Original related images:
  - scientist portrait, manuscript/letters, paper/patent first page, experimental device or lab photo.
- Event related images:
  - result/discovery diagrams, institution or location images.
- Secondary creative images:
  - info cards, time capsule cards, light meme visuals.

### 8.2 Image requirements
- Relevance:
  - each image must directly support person/event/context or strengthen emotional resonance.
- Traceability and usage:
  - prioritize public domain, CC, or official reusable assets.
  - provide image credit and usage/license notes for every image.
